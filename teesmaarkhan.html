Tees Maar Khan (2010)

Storyline: Tees Maar Khan is a colorful and over-the-top heist comedy centered around Tabrez Mirza Khan, famously known as Tees Maar Khan — a notorious conman who sees himself as an unstoppable and legendary criminal. Raised on a steady diet of crime movies and tales of clever tricksters, Khan believes in living a life full of adventure, scams, and non-stop thrills. The film kicks off with his daring escapes and audacious cons across the world, making him a nightmare for the police. When the Johri Brothers, two crooked antique smugglers, approach Tees Maar Khan with the task of stealing a priceless shipment of British antiques guarded on a heavily fortified moving train, he sees this as his ultimate challenge — and his greatest opportunity to cement his legacy. To execute the grand heist, Khan comes up with an outrageous plan: he decides to stage a fake film shoot in a small village along the train’s route, using the villagers as unknowing accomplices. Enlisting the help of his self-absorbed, aspiring actress girlfriend Anya, and Bollywood superstar Aatish Kapoor (who is desperate for an Oscar), Khan manipulates everyone around him into believing they are part of a grand epic movie production called "Bharat Ka Khazana." Meanwhile, he trains the villagers as if they were acting in a scene, while secretly preparing them to unknowingly participate in the real robbery. Amidst crazy rehearsals, hilarious misunderstandings, and laughable moments of “filmy drama,” the plan begins to take shape. Aatish Kapoor, convinced that his “performance” will win him an Oscar, throws himself fully into his role, adding to the chaos. As the day of the heist approaches, unforeseen obstacles, police suspicions, and the villagers' growing questions create mounting tension. What follows is a series of absurd, high-energy sequences where nothing goes as smoothly as planned. Tees Maar Khan must juggle the egos of a star, the innocence of the villagers, the sharp instincts of suspicious cops, and his own larger-than-life ambitions. In a hilarious and unpredictable climax, the truth comes dangerously close to being exposed. Packed with slapstick comedy, outrageous performances, colorful visuals, and chartbuster songs like “Sheila Ki Jawani,” Tees Maar Khan delivers a wild, fast-paced cinematic experience. Despite mixed critical reception, the film is remembered for its unabashed craziness, high-energy dance numbers, and Akshay Kumar’s over-the-top comic performance as the lovable rogue who dreams big, scams bigger, and somehow always manages to escape trouble — almost like a true Bollywood hero.
