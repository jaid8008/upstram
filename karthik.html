Karthik Calling Karthik (2010)

Storyline: *Karthik Calling Karthik* is a psychological thriller that explores themes of mental illness, identity, and self-worth through the life of a socially awkward, underconfident man named Karthik Narayan. Living a dull, unnoticed life as a timid employee in a construction firm, Karthik is constantly belittled by his boss and invisible to the people around him, including his beautiful and ambitious colleague, Shonali, whom he silently admires. Haunted by a traumatic childhood incident involving his brother's death — for which he secretly blames himself — Karthik suffers from intense guilt, loneliness, and low self-esteem. One day, on the verge of giving up, his life changes with a mysterious phone call — from someone who claims to be none other than "Karthik" himself. The voice on the other end is calm, assertive, and encouraging, claiming to be his inner voice, reaching out to help him reclaim control over his life. Skeptical at first, Karthik begins to follow the caller’s advice — waking up early, standing up for himself, excelling at work, and even finding the confidence to pursue a romantic relationship with Shonali. His transformation is remarkable. He earns a promotion, finds happiness, and begins to live a life full of purpose and joy. But soon, the calls start becoming increasingly controlling and cryptic. Karthik’s reliance on them begins to spiral into obsession. When he shares this secret with Shonali and his psychiatrist, they fear for his mental stability. The phone calls begin to torment him, warning him against disclosing their existence and threatening to destroy everything he has rebuilt. As paranoia sets in, the film gradually peels back layers of Karthik’s psychological condition. Through his therapy sessions and mounting internal conflict, it is revealed that Karthik suffers from dissociative identity disorder — and the calls were never from an external source but manifestations of his subconscious mind, recorded and played back to himself in a deeply complex mental feedback loop. His journey becomes one of confronting his trauma, accepting his fractured mind, and learning to live without the crutches of his alter ego. In a quietly powerful climax, Karthik undergoes therapy, embraces his past, and finds a way to reclaim his identity — this time without depending on a mysterious voice. He finds work again, and even reconnects with Shonali, but on healthy terms, showing how far he’s come. *Karthik Calling Karthik* is a haunting and emotional exploration of mental illness and self-discovery. With a compelling performance by Farhan Akhtar and a balanced mix of suspense, romance, and psychological tension, the film offers a rare mainstream take on schizophrenia and inner conflict. The atmospheric direction, gripping music, and clever storytelling make it a unique film that lingers in the mind long after the credits roll.
