Ratsasan (2018) – Hindi Dubbed as Gumnaam

Storyline: *Ratsasan* (translated as "Demon") is a gripping psychological crime thriller that follows Arun Kumar, an aspiring filmmaker who is forced to become a police officer after the tragic death of his father. His dreams of making a movie about serial killers come to an abrupt halt, but destiny leads him down a more terrifying path — one where he must face a real-life monster. Soon after joining the police force, Arun is pulled into the investigation of a series of brutal murders targeting schoolgirls. The victims are all young, innocent girls, and their bodies are left in horrifying conditions. With little evidence and growing public fear, Arun begins to see connections that others have missed, drawing from the knowledge he had gathered while researching serial killers for his now-abandoned film project. As Arun delves deeper, the trail becomes increasingly complex and disturbing. Clues point towards someone with a twisted mind and a brutal motive. Amidst police bureaucracy, pressure from the media, and personal threats, Arun races against time to unmask the killer before more lives are lost. The investigation leads him into the psychological profile of the killer — a deeply scarred individual with a disturbing past. Meanwhile, Arun develops a close bond with Viji, a kind-hearted teacher, and her niece Kayal, who plays a pivotal emotional role in his life. But the killer gets bolder, and the circle of danger tightens around those Arun cares for. The film’s climax is a tense, emotionally charged confrontation that reveals the killer’s horrific backstory — involving a childhood disability, rejection, and psychological trauma that mutated into an insatiable urge for revenge. The narrative expertly weaves past and present, revealing how cruelty, bullying, and societal neglect can create real-life demons. *Ratsasan* is praised for its nail-biting tension, clever screenplay, and disturbing realism. Ghibran’s haunting background score enhances the fear, while Vishnu Vishal delivers a career-defining performance. The film’s pacing, coupled with shocking reveals and an emotional core, makes it one of the most acclaimed thrillers in Indian cinema in recent years. In its Hindi dubbed version, often titled *Gumnaam* on streaming platforms, the film reached a wider audience and gained cult popularity for its chilling narrative, powerful direction, and immersive suspense.
