Trapped (2017)

Storyline: *Trapped* is a gripping survival thriller that takes an ordinary urban setting and turns it into a claustrophobic nightmare. The story revolves around Shaurya, a mild-mannered and slightly awkward young man living in Mumbai. He works a regular job and has recently fallen in love with Noorie, a co-worker who is about to be married off in an arranged match. In a desperate bid to be with her, Shaurya impulsively proposes that they move in together, and she agrees. Eager to impress, Shaurya hastily finds an apartment in a high-rise building that appears perfect at first glance — spacious, affordable, and unoccupied. But what he doesn’t realize is that the building is still under construction, largely vacant, and managed poorly. The very next morning, disaster strikes. Shaurya accidentally locks himself inside the flat with no phone, no water, minimal food, and no one aware of his exact whereabouts. To make matters worse, a power outage disables the elevator and security systems, leaving him completely isolated. As the days pass, panic turns to desperation. With no tools, no electricity, and no mobile signal, Shaurya’s only companion becomes the eerie silence of the building. He battles extreme hunger, dehydration, rats, pigeons, and above all — his own mind. What begins as a minor inconvenience turns into a harrowing test of human will as he struggles to survive with limited resources and mounting fear. The film follows his mental and physical deterioration in raw detail. Each attempt to escape — whether climbing the building, signaling neighbors, or trying to break the lock — fails in terrifying or frustrating ways. His hallucinations, internal monologues, and breakdowns add layers of emotional depth as we watch his transformation from a soft-spoken office worker to a primal survivor. Yet, even in his bleakest moments, Shaurya clings to hope. His love for Noorie and the basic instinct to survive push him to the limits. When he finally manages to escape, emaciated and broken, he steps back into a world that moved on without realizing he was ever missing. The ending is subtle, introspective, and deeply haunting. *Trapped* is a rare kind of thriller — one that doesn't rely on loud action or villains, but on the terrifying possibility of being forgotten in a modern city. With minimal dialogue and a single actor carrying the weight of the story, Rajkummar Rao delivers one of the most powerful performances of his career. Director Vikramaditya Motwane crafts a tense, minimalist film that explores isolation, resilience, and the terrifying fragility of our modern lives. It's not just a survival story — it's a psychological exploration of loneliness, fear, and the human will to live.
